About Us

Yamuna, often referred to as "Maa Yamuna" or "Mother Yamuna," is a sacred river in Hinduism, worshipped as a goddess. She is also known as Jumna or Yami and is considered the second most sacred river in India, after the Ganges. Yamuna is the daughter of the sun god Surya and the sister of the god of death, Yama. She is revered for her role in Krishna's life, and her waters are believed to have purifying and rejuvenating properties, relieving the pain of death. Approximately 57 million people depend on Yamuna's water
